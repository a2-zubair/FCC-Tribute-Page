About Zainul Abedin

Zainul Abedin (29 December 1914 – 28 May 1976) was a Bengali painter. He got the break through in 1944 with his Famine Series paintings of 1943. After partition he moved to Pakistan; and finally when Bangladesh was created in 1971, he was rightly considered by Syed Manzoorul Islam as the founding father of Bangladeshi modern art. In 1948 he helped establish the Institute of Arts and Crafts (now Faculty of Fine Arts) at Dhaka University. The Indian Express described him as a Legendary Bangladeshi painter. Like many of his contemporaries, his paintings on the Bengal famine of 1943 is probably his most characteristic work.He was given the title Shilpacharya (Great Teacher of the Arts) in Bangladesh for his artistic and visionary qualities. Famous Arts of Zainul Abedin

In 1943, he drew a series of sketches on the man-made famine that had spread throughout Bengal, killing hundreds of thousands of people.

He was involved in the Bengali Language Movement of East Pakistan. Abedin was involved in the Bangladesh liberation war movement.

Great works of art often emanate inherent feelings and/or emotions otherwise difficult to be captured in other forms of expression.
